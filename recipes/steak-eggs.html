Steak and Eggs

Description

Steak and Eggs is a classic, protein-packed meal—tender, thin-cut steak served with perfectly cooked eggs. Vered DeLeeuw’s version is quick (about 20 minutes), easy to make any time—breakfast, dinner, or brunch—and utterly satisfying :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Ingredients

  • 6 oz (170 g) thin-cut steak (e.g., New York strip), ~½″ thick
  • Sea salt, divided
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper, divided
  • Avocado oil spray (or neutral oil/butter for frying)
  • 2 large eggs
  • Optional garnish: pinch of red pepper flakes, chopped parsley :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Instructions

  1. Preheat an indoor grill or skillet. Season both sides of the steak with salt and pepper :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.
  2. Spray skillet with avocado oil and sear steak 2 minutes per side for medium‑rare (or 3 min each side on electric grill) :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.
  3. Transfer steak to a plate and let it rest while you cook the eggs.
  4. In the same pan (or a separate non-stick skillet), cook eggs sunny-side up until whites are set but yolks remain runny.
  5. Plate steak and eggs together. Sprinkle with salt, black pepper, optional red pepper flakes, and parsley.
