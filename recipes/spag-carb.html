Spaghetti Carbonara Recipe

Description

Spaghetti alla Carbonara is a classic Roman pasta dish made with pancetta, eggs, Parmesan, Pecorino, and black pepper. No creamâ€”just a rich, creamy sauce created from the emulsification of pasta water and egg-cheese mixture. Simple, fast, and incredibly flavorful, this dish is comfort food at its finest.

Ingredients

  • 100g pancetta
  • 50g Pecorino cheese
  • 50g Parmesan cheese
  • 3 large eggs
  • 350g spaghetti
  • 2 plump garlic cloves, peeled and left whole
  • 50g unsalted butter
  • Sea salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Put a large saucepan of water on to boil.
  2. Finely chop the pancetta (remove rind), grate both cheeses, and mix them together.
  3. Beat the eggs in a bowl and season with black pepper. Set aside.
  4. Add 1 tsp salt to boiling water. Cook the spaghetti for about 10 minutes or until al dente.
  5. Bruise the garlic cloves with the side of a knife.
  6. While pasta cooks, melt butter in a large pan and fry pancetta with garlic for ~5 minutes until golden and crisp. Discard garlic.
  7. Keep pancetta on low heat. Use tongs to transfer cooked spaghetti into the pancetta pan. Reserve pasta water.
  8. Mix most of the cheese into the beaten eggs, saving a small handful for garnish.
  9. Remove pan from heat. Quickly pour in egg-cheese mixture, and toss the pasta so it coats without scrambling.
  10. Add a few tablespoons of reserved pasta water to create a silky sauce. Season with salt if needed.
  11. Plate the pasta by twisting with a fork. Top with remaining cheese and a final grind of black pepper.
  12. If the dish dries out before serving, add a bit more hot pasta water to revive the sauce.
